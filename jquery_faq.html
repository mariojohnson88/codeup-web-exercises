National Park Fun Facts

Yellowstone National Park
It is the world's first national park.
Black Canyon of the Gunnison National Park
Colorado's biggest cliff is the Black Canyon's Painted Wall
Glacier Bay National Park and Preserve
Glacier Bay National Park only encompasses 1% of Alaska's land.
Big Bend National Park
There are more species of birds found in Big Bend National Park than in any other national park in the U.S.
Lassen Volcanic National Park
The last major eruptions of Lassen Peak occurred between 1915 and 1917.
Death Valley National Park
Death Valley is home to the Timbisha tribe of Native Americans, formerly known as the Panamint Shoshone, who have inhabited the valley for at least the past millennium.
Wrangell-St. Elias National Park and Preserve
The park extends more than 5.3 million hectares.
Gates of the Arctic National Park and Preserve
Gates of the Arctic National Park no roads within it and this makes it less popular with tourists. Only a little over 12,660 visitors entered the park in 2014.
Dry Tortugas National Park
Dry Tortugas National Park is comprised of 99% water. The remaining 1% is made up of seven major islands and 104 acres of land in total.
Kenai Fjords national Park
Orca whales, fin whales, minke whales, humpback whales, Pacific white-sided dolphins, and Dall's porpoises can be found in the waters of Kenai Fjords National Park.
