National Park Fun Facts

Yellowstone National Park
It is the world's first national park.
Black Canyon of the Gunnison National Park
Colorado's biggest cliff is the Black Canyon's Painted Wall
Glacier Bay National Park and Preserve
Glacier Bay National Park only encompasses 1% of Alaska's land.
Big Bend National Park
There are more species of birds found in Big Bend National Park than in any other national park in the U.S.
Lassen Volcanic National Park
The last major eruptions of Lassen Peak occurred between 1915 and 1917.
Death Valley National Park
Death Valley is home to the Timbisha tribe of Native Americans, formerly known as the Panamint Shoshone, who have inhabited the valley for at least the past millennium.
Wrangell-St. Elias National Park and Preserve
The park extends more than 5.3 million hectares.
Gates of the Arctic National Park and Preserve
Gates of the Arctic National Park no roads within it and this makes it less popular with tourists. Only a little over 12,660 visitors entered the park in 2014.
Dry Tortugas National Park
Dry Tortugas National Park is comprised of 99% water. The remaining 1% is made up of seven major islands and 104 acres of land in total.
Kenai Fjords national Park
Orca whales, fin whales, minke whales, humpback whales, Pacific white-sided dolphins, and Dall's porpoises can be found in the waters of Kenai Fjords National Park.

More Wrangell-St. Elias National Park Facts

  • Wildlife that can be found in Wrangell-St. Elias National Park include Alaskan moose, Dall sheep, black bears, grizzly bears, bison, caribou, mountain goats, arctic ground squirrels, porcupines, red foxes, snowshoe hares, lynxes, martens, river otters, coyotes, hoary marmots, weasels, voles, pikas, wolverines, wolves, and many others including the possibility of cougars.
  • Wilderness recreation in Wrangell-St. Elias National Park can include hiking, camping, hunting, fishing, and the use of a variety of vehicles such as airplanes and snowmobiles to get around.
  • In the coastal waters of Wrangell-St. Elias National Park it is possible to view sea lions, whales, and harbor seals.
  • Between 1903 and 1938 the park's center - a boomtown called Kennecott - exploded because of the areas rich copper deposit discovery. It is considered to be one of the richest deposits in the world. The buildings of this abandoned town make up a National Historic Landmark district, declared as such in 1986.

More Yellow Stone National Park Facts

  • For centuries, Native Americans came to Obsidian Cliff to quarry obsidian, which they traded for projectiles and other tools. Obsidian from this site in Yellowstone National Park has been found as far east as Ohio. Obsidian Cliff is a National Historic Monument.
  • Yellowstone has the largest, free-roaming herd of bison in the world
  • After two decades of extinction, gray wolves were restored to Yellowstone National Park in 1995
  • Yellowstone’s Old Faithful did at one time erupt at an average of every 60 minutes. Due to earthquake damage to its “plumbing” over the years, the geyser now “performs” every 88 minutes (+/- 10 minutes on average), with a minimum gap of just under an hour and a maximum gap of three hours.

More Big Bend National Park Facts

  • Animals found within Big Bend National Park include Mexican black bears, roadrunners, coyotes, cougars, kangaroo rats, gray foxes, black-tailed jackrabbits, and javelinas. Most animals are nocturnal (come out at night)
  • Throughout history there have been a variety of Native tribes and explorers that have passed through the region now called Big Bend National Park. These people have included the Apache Indians, Comanche Indians, Spanish explorers, soldiers and revolutionaries from Mexico and the United States, outlaws, ranchers, and farmers.
  • The Chisos Mountains are the only mountain range in the United States to be completely contained within a national park. Chisos Mountains are also the most southern United States mountain range.
  • Langford Hot Springs are located in Big Bend National Park. The temperature of the spring is approximately 105 degrees Fahrenheit.
